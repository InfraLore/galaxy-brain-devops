# My recollection:

Here's what I remember... I was helping a user convert a heavy GitHub Actions workflow over to a container-based workflow. The
  original workflow built a Docker image and pushed it to ECR. It was working well, but was very slow and resource-intensive. My goal
  was to move some of the repetitive steps into a container, so he could re-use that container. He was skeptical but willing to try.
  As soon as he encountered ECR login failures, though, he was ready to abandon the idea. Then, he remembered that we previously
  had to adjust something about "hops" in order to get ECR logins to work on his original workflow. So, I pulled up this management
  harness, and then fired Claude up, and asked you to review the project, and then tell me if you could see anything about "hops"
   in it. And Claude very excitedly (as is his way) said *yes!* and told me about an imds debug target. And then we had a nice
  convesation that lead to a pull request and a fix that enabled the new container-based workflows to complete, and a happy customer.

# Claude's recap:

1. You asked me to look for anything about "hops" — I found debug-imds and got excited about it, yes.
  2. We figured out the 3-hop dind chain together (inner container → dind daemon → pod network → host NIC).
  3. I built set-imds-hop-limit and set-launch-template-hop-limit, but your SSO role (managed-projectuser) didn't have
  ec2:ModifyInstanceMetadataOptions — so patching live nodes was a dead end.
  4. We pivoted to baking hop limit 3 into a new launch template version (LT version 29) and recycling the nodes instead.
  5. Along the way I built the recycle-node target interactively — including discovering that pods-on-node already existed, so we used
  that to check for in-flight jobs first.
  6. There was genuine suspense: node 1 drained and terminated, then you sat there watching make list-nodes waiting for a fresh node to
   appear. I told you it was normal and would take 3-5 minutes.
  7. Then node 2 got recycled the same way, and all nodes came up at hop limit 3.
